Deep learning is a subfield of machine learning that uses artificial neural networks with many layers to model and understand complex patterns in data.
It has emerged as one of the most powerful approaches in artificial intelligence, achieving remarkable results in areas such as image recognition, speech processing, and natural language understanding.
The term deep refers to the number of layers in the neural network, with deeper networks generally being capable of learning more abstract representations.

A neural network is composed of interconnected nodes or neurons organized into layers.
The input layer receives raw data, hidden layers perform transformations, and the output layer produces the final prediction.
During training, the network adjusts the weights of connections between neurons to minimize the difference between predicted and actual outputs.

Convolutional neural networks, or CNNs, are a type of deep learning architecture specifically designed for processing grid-like data such as images.
They use convolutional layers to automatically learn spatial hierarchies of features, from simple edges and textures to complex objects.
CNNs have achieved superhuman performance on image classification benchmarks and are widely used in computer vision applications.

Recurrent neural networks, or RNNs, are designed for sequential data such as text and time series.
They have a form of memory that allows information to persist across time steps.
Long short-term memory networks, or LSTMs, are a popular type of RNN that can capture long-range dependencies in sequential data.

Training deep learning models requires large amounts of labeled data and significant computational resources.
Graphics processing units, or GPUs, are commonly used to accelerate the training process by performing many calculations in parallel.
Transfer learning allows practitioners to reuse pretrained models on new tasks, reducing the need for large datasets.

Deep learning has driven breakthroughs in autonomous driving, medical image analysis, drug discovery, and generative AI.
Models like generative adversarial networks can create realistic images, videos, and audio from scratch.
As research in deep learning continues to advance, new architectures and techniques are constantly being developed to push the boundaries of what is possible.
