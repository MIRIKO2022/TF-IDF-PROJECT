Natural language processing, commonly known as NLP, is a field of artificial intelligence that focuses on the interaction between computers and human language.
It involves developing algorithms and models that allow computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language in a meaningful way.
NLP combines insights from linguistics, computer science, and machine learning to tackle the complexity of human communication.

One of the fundamental challenges in NLP is that human language is inherently ambiguous and context-dependent.
The same word can have different meanings depending on the sentence, and the same idea can be expressed in countless different ways.
NLP systems must be able to handle this variability and ambiguity to be truly effective.

Text preprocessing is a crucial first step in most NLP pipelines.
This involves cleaning and transforming raw text data into a format that machine learning models can process.
Common preprocessing steps include tokenization, stop word removal, stemming, and lemmatization.

Tokenization involves splitting text into individual words or subword units called tokens.
Stop word removal filters out common words like the, is, and at that carry little semantic meaning.
Stemming and lemmatization reduce words to their base or root form to normalize the vocabulary.

Part-of-speech tagging assigns grammatical labels to each word in a sentence, such as noun, verb, or adjective.
Named entity recognition identifies and classifies named entities in text, such as people, organizations, and locations.
Sentiment analysis determines the emotional tone of a piece of text, whether it is positive, negative, or neutral.

Modern NLP has been revolutionized by transformer-based models such as BERT and GPT.
These models are trained on massive amounts of text data and can capture complex linguistic patterns.
They have achieved state-of-the-art performance on a wide range of NLP tasks including question answering, text summarization, and machine translation.
